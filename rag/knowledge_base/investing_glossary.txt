TITLE: General Investing Glossary

Diversification: spreading investments across multiple assets, sectors, or
asset classes so that a poor outcome in any single holding has a limited
effect on the overall portfolio. Diversification reduces unsystematic
(company-specific) risk but does not eliminate systematic (market-wide)
risk.

Sharpe Ratio: a measure of risk-adjusted return, calculated as the excess
return of an investment over a risk-free rate, divided by the investment's
volatility (standard deviation of returns). A higher Sharpe Ratio indicates
more return earned per unit of risk taken.

Efficient Frontier: in Modern Portfolio Theory, the set of portfolios that
offer the highest expected return for a given level of risk (or the lowest
risk for a given expected return). Portfolios below the frontier are
considered sub-optimal, since a different mix could achieve the same return
with less risk.

Modern Portfolio Theory (MPT): a framework, developed by Harry Markowitz,
for constructing a portfolio that maximizes expected return for a given
level of risk by considering not just individual asset risk, but how assets
move in relation to each other (correlation).

Dollar-Cost Averaging: investing a fixed amount of money at regular
intervals regardless of price, which reduces the impact of short-term price
volatility on the average purchase price over time.

None of these concepts guarantee investment outcomes. They are frameworks
for reasoning about risk and return, not predictions.
