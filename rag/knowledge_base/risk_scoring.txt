TITLE: How Risk Scoring Works in FinSight AI

FinSight AI's risk module estimates a 0-100 risk score for a stock based on
several factors derived from its recent historical price behavior:

Volatility: how much the stock's daily returns fluctuate. A stock whose
price swings widely day to day is scored as higher risk than one that moves
gradually.

Maximum drawdown: the largest peak-to-trough decline observed in the
lookback period. A stock that has previously fallen sharply from a recent
high is scored as carrying more downside risk.

Beta-like sensitivity: how much the stock's movements tend to be larger or
smaller than typical market-wide swings over the same period.

These factors are combined into a single score, and a corresponding
qualitative label (for example, Low, Moderate, High, or Very High risk) is
shown alongside it. This score reflects only historical price statistics —
it does not know about a company's balance sheet, debt levels, competitive
position, or business fundamentals, and it does not account for
company-specific events that haven't yet shown up in the price history.

Risk and expected return are related but different concepts: a stock can be
volatile (high risk score) while also being expected to trend upward, or
can be low-volatility while still carrying business risk that a purely
price-based score won't capture. Risk scores should be read as one input
among several, not a complete picture of an investment's safety.
